Last Tuesday the leadership team met to discuss several topics. The meeting started at 2pm and ran until about 4:30pm. Present were Minh (CTO), Linh (VP Engineering), Hoa (VP Product), Duc (Head of Data), and Trang (HR Director).

Minh kicked off with an update on the Q4 hiring plan. He said we need to hire 15 more engineers before end of year, specifically 6 backend engineers, 4 ML engineers, 3 frontend engineers, and 2 DevOps engineers. The budget for this is already approved at 3.2 billion VND total compensation. Linh mentioned that the current pipeline has about 40 candidates in various interview stages, but conversion rate has been low at around 22%. She suggested we might need to increase the referral bonus from 10 million to 15 million VND to improve quality of candidates. Minh agreed to bring this up with the CFO.

Then we moved on to the infrastructure migration discussion. Duc presented the plan to migrate from our current on-premise Hadoop cluster to a cloud-based data lakehouse architecture. The migration is expected to take 6 months and cost approximately 8.5 billion VND in total, including 3.2 billion for cloud infrastructure setup, 2.8 billion for data migration and validation, and 2.5 billion for team training and process updates. The key concern raised by Linh was the risk of data loss during migration. Duc explained that they plan to run both systems in parallel for 3 months before cutting over, and all data will be checksummed before and after transfer. Minh asked about the expected cost savings post-migration, and Duc estimated annual savings of 4.2 billion VND from reduced hardware maintenance, improved query performance, and elimination of 3 full-time infrastructure admin positions.

Hoa then brought up the product roadmap for Q4. The main features planned are: a new customer dashboard with real-time analytics (target launch: October 15), an AI-powered recommendation engine for content personalization (target: November 1), integration with Salesforce CRM for enterprise customers (target: November 15), and a self-service billing portal (target: December 1). Hoa emphasized that the recommendation engine is the highest priority because three enterprise customers have explicitly requested it and one of them (VinGroup) has made it a condition for renewing their 2.8 billion VND annual contract. Linh raised concerns about the timeline being too aggressive given the current hiring situation. After discussion, the team agreed to focus engineering resources on the recommendation engine first and potentially push the Salesforce integration to Q1 2026.

The conversation shifted to the recent production incident from last Friday. A database connection pool exhaustion caused 45 minutes of downtime for the analytics dashboard, affecting approximately 800 users. The root cause was identified as a memory leak in the connection pooling library version 2.4.1 that manifests under sustained high load. The fix was to upgrade to version 2.5.0 and implement connection timeout limits. Linh reported that the incident response time was 8 minutes from first alert to engineer on-call being paged, but the actual fix took 37 minutes because the on-call engineer was unfamiliar with the connection pooling configuration. She proposed implementing monthly incident response drills and creating a runbook specifically for database-related incidents. Minh approved both initiatives and asked Linh to have the runbook ready by end of August.

Trang gave an update on the employee satisfaction survey results. Overall satisfaction score was 7.2 out of 10, down from 7.8 in the previous quarter. The main areas of concern were: work-life balance (scored 5.8, down from 6.9), career growth opportunities (scored 6.4, down from 7.1), and compensation competitiveness (scored 6.1, unchanged). The highest-rated areas were team collaboration (8.5) and quality of technical challenges (8.3). Trang noted that the work-life balance decline correlates with the push to deliver the Q3 product releases and recommended implementing mandatory no-meeting Wednesdays and limiting after-hours Slack notifications. Minh expressed concern about the trend and asked Trang to prepare a detailed action plan by next Friday.

The team also briefly discussed the upcoming company offsite planned for September 20-22 in Da Nang. The budget is 850 million VND for 180 employees, covering flights, hotel, team activities, and the annual town hall. Trang mentioned that 92% of employees have confirmed attendance. The agenda includes a strategy presentation by the CEO, department breakout sessions, and a team-building activity at Ba Na Hills.

Finally, Minh mentioned that the board meeting is scheduled for August 8. He needs the Q2 financial summary, the updated product roadmap, and the hiring progress report from each department head by August 1. He also reminded everyone about the new security compliance requirements from the information security team — all production systems must complete the SOC 2 Type II audit by end of Q3, and any team that handles customer PII must complete the updated data handling training by August 15.
