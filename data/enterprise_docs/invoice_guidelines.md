## Invoice Processing and Payment Guidelines

**Effective Date:** April 1, 2025  
**Last Revised:** June 20, 2025  
**Policy Owner:** Accounts Payable, Finance Department, Gravity Engineering Solutions JSC  
**Applies To:** All vendors, subcontractors, and internal staff involved in invoice processing  
**Document ID:** FIN-AP-2025-008

---

### 1. Overview

This document establishes the standard procedures for issuing, receiving, and processing invoices at Gravity Engineering Solutions JSC. All invoices must comply with Vietnamese tax regulations, particularly **Circular 78/2021/TT-BTC** on electronic invoices, and must be processed through the company's approved e-invoice system.

Timely and accurate invoice processing is essential for maintaining vendor relationships, ensuring tax compliance, and supporting accurate financial reporting.

---

### 2. Payment Terms

| Client/Vendor Type        | Standard Payment Terms | Grace Period |
|---------------------------|------------------------|--------------|
| Domestic (Vietnam-based)  | Net 30 days            | 5 days       |
| International             | Net 45 days            | 7 days       |

- **Net 30:** Payment is due within 30 calendar days from the invoice date for all domestic vendors and clients.
- **Net 45:** Payment is due within 45 calendar days from the invoice date for all international vendors and clients, reflecting additional processing time for cross-border transfers and currency conversion.
- Payment terms begin from the date a valid, complete invoice is received by the Accounts Payable team, not from the date of service delivery.
- Non-standard payment terms (e.g., Net 15, milestone-based, or progress payments) must be pre-approved by the CFO and documented in the signed contract.

For project-based engagements exceeding 500,000,000 VND (500M), the standard payment schedule is: 30% upon contract signing, 40% at project midpoint milestone, and 30% upon final delivery and acceptance.

### 3. Invoice Requirements

Every invoice submitted to or issued by Gravity Engineering Solutions must include the following information:

#### 3.1 Mandatory Fields

- **Purchase Order (PO) Number:** Every invoice must reference a valid PO number issued by the Procurement department. Invoices without a PO number will be returned to the sender and will not be processed.
- **Tax Code (Ma so thue):** The vendor's Vietnamese tax identification number. For international vendors, the equivalent tax registration number or business registration number is required.
- **Bank Details:** Payment will only be made to verified bank accounts. Accepted banks:
  - **Vietcombank** (Joint Stock Commercial Bank for Foreign Trade of Vietnam)
  - **ACB** (Asia Commercial Joint Stock Bank)
  - Other banks may be accepted on a case-by-case basis with Finance Department approval and an additional 3 business days for verification.
- **Itemized Services:** Each line item must include a clear description of the service or product, quantity, unit price, and total amount. Lump-sum invoices without itemization will be rejected.
- **Invoice Number:** Unique sequential number following the vendor's numbering scheme.
- **Invoice Date:** Must not be more than 5 business days before or after the service delivery date.
- **Company Legal Name and Address:** Full registered legal name, not trade names or abbreviations.
- **Currency:** VND for domestic transactions; USD, AUD, or SGD for international transactions. The applicable exchange rate source (Vietcombank selling rate) must be noted for non-VND invoices.

#### 3.2 Additional Requirements for E-Invoices

All domestic invoices must be issued as electronic invoices (hoa don dien tu) through an approved e-invoice service provider. Gravity Engineering Solutions uses **VNInvoice** as its primary e-invoice platform, fully compliant with **Circular 78/2021/TT-BTC** issued by the Ministry of Finance.

- E-invoices must carry a valid digital signature from the issuing entity.
- The e-invoice XML must be transmitted to the General Department of Taxation (GDT) portal within 24 hours of issuance.
- Paper invoices are no longer accepted for domestic transactions as of January 1, 2023, per government mandate.
- International vendors not subject to Vietnamese e-invoice requirements may submit PDF invoices via email to ap@company.com, provided all mandatory fields are included.

### 4. Value Added Tax (VAT)

| Category                  | VAT Rate | Notes                                    |
|---------------------------|----------|------------------------------------------|
| Professional Services     | 10%      | Engineering, consulting, project management |
| Software Licenses         | 8%       | Per Resolution 43/2022/QH15 extended rate |
| Hardware / Equipment      | 10%      | Standard rate                            |
| Training Services         | 10%      | Standard rate                            |
| Exported Services         | 0%       | Services performed for foreign entities outside Vietnam |

- VAT must be itemized separately on all invoices. Invoices that bundle VAT into the total without a separate line will be returned for correction.
- For mixed invoices containing both 10% and 8% VAT items, each rate must be applied to the respective line items with subtotals clearly shown.
- Input VAT credits are claimed monthly by the Finance Department. Vendors must ensure e-invoices are correctly registered with the GDT to enable credit claims.
- Withholding tax obligations for international vendors (typically 5% for services) are handled by the Finance Department and deducted from payment as required by Vietnamese tax law.

### 5. Late Payment Penalties

- A **late payment penalty of 0.05% per day** is applied to overdue invoices after the applicable grace period (5 days domestic, 7 days international).
- The penalty is calculated on the outstanding principal amount, excluding VAT.
- Maximum cumulative penalty is capped at 8% of the invoice value.
- Penalty waivers may be granted by the CFO for documented delays caused by force majeure, banking system outages, or invoice disputes under active resolution.

**Example calculation:**  
Invoice amount: 100,000,000 VND (100M), domestic, Net 30 terms.  
Payment made on Day 42 (7 days past grace period):  
Penalty = 100,000,000 x 0.05% x 7 = 350,000 VND.

### 6. Dispute Resolution

- Invoice disputes must be raised via email to **finance@company.com** within **7 calendar days** of invoice receipt.
- The email must include: invoice number, PO number, specific items in dispute, supporting documentation, and proposed resolution.
- The Finance Department will acknowledge disputes within 2 business days and provide a resolution or escalation path within 10 business days.
- Disputed amounts are placed on hold and excluded from late payment penalty calculations until resolution.
- If a dispute cannot be resolved at the Finance Department level, it is escalated to the CFO, and if necessary, to the dispute resolution mechanism specified in the underlying contract (typically arbitration at the Vietnam International Arbitration Centre - VIAC).

### 7. Invoice Processing Workflow

| Step | Action                              | Responsible Party      | SLA              |
|------|-------------------------------------|------------------------|------------------|
| 1    | Invoice received and logged         | Accounts Payable       | Same business day|
| 2    | PO matching and validation          | Accounts Payable       | 2 business days  |
| 3    | Service delivery confirmation       | Project Manager        | 3 business days  |
| 4    | Approval per delegation of authority| Department Head / CFO  | 2 business days  |
| 5    | Payment scheduling                  | Accounts Payable       | Next payment run |
| 6    | Payment execution                   | Treasury               | Per payment terms|
| 7    | Payment confirmation to vendor      | Accounts Payable       | 1 business day   |

Payment runs are executed every **Tuesday and Thursday**. Invoices approved by 12:00 PM on the day before a payment run will be included in that run. The company maintains a minimum of 2 payment runs per week to ensure timely vendor payments.

### 8. Vendor Onboarding for Payment

New vendors must complete the Vendor Registration Form (FIN-F-015) and provide:
- Business registration certificate (Giay chung nhan dang ky doanh nghiep)
- Tax registration certificate
- Bank account verification letter from Vietcombank or ACB
- Signed standard terms and conditions
- W-8BEN or W-8BEN-E for international vendors receiving USD payments

Vendor onboarding typically requires 5-7 business days. First payments to new vendors undergo additional verification by the Treasury team and may require an extra 3 business days.
