## Performance Review Policy - GravityTech Vietnam JSC

**Document ID:** HR-PERF-004  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Last Revised:** February 20, 2025  
**Approved By:** Nguyen Thi Mai, Chief People Officer  

---

### 1. Purpose

This policy establishes a fair, transparent, and consistent process for evaluating employee performance at GravityTech Vietnam. Performance reviews are designed to recognize achievements, identify development opportunities, and align individual contributions with company objectives.

---

### 2. Review Cadence

GravityTech operates a **continuous performance management** model with formal review touchpoints throughout the year.

| Review Type | Frequency | Timing | Participants |
|-------------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Quarterly Check-In | Every 3 months | Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct (first 2 weeks) | Employee + Direct Manager |
| Annual Performance Review | Annually | **April 1 - April 15** | Employee + Manager + HR |
| 360-Degree Feedback | Annually (during April review) | March 15 - March 31 | Employee + Peers + Reports (L3+ only) |
| Probation Review | At Day 90 | Varies by start date | Employee + Manager + HR |

#### 2.1 Quarterly Check-Ins

- Quarterly check-ins are **30-45 minute conversations** between the employee and their direct manager.
- They are forward-looking and focus on: progress toward goals, blockers, development needs, and well-being.
- Check-ins are documented using the **Quarterly Check-In Template** in BambooHR.
- While check-ins do not produce a formal rating, the manager should note any concerns or exceptional performance for reference during the annual review.
- Employees who miss 2 or more quarterly check-ins in a year will be flagged for follow-up by HR.

---

### 3. Annual Performance Review Process

The annual review is the primary mechanism for formal performance evaluation, compensation decisions, and promotion recommendations. The review cycle runs from **March 1 to April 15** each year, evaluating performance for the preceding calendar year (January 1 - December 31).

#### Step 1: Self-Assessment (March 1 - March 15)

- Every employee completes a **self-assessment** in BambooHR covering:
  - Achievement against goals set in the previous review.
  - Key contributions and impact.
  - Areas for improvement.
  - Career development aspirations.
- Self-assessments should include **specific examples and measurable outcomes** wherever possible.

#### Step 2: 360-Degree Feedback (March 15 - March 31)

- **Applicable to L3 (Senior) and above.**
- Employees at L3+ nominate **3-5 peers** and **all direct reports** for 360-degree feedback.
- The manager may add up to 2 additional reviewers (e.g., cross-functional partners).
- Feedback is collected via an anonymous survey with both rated questions (1-5 scale) and open-ended comments.
- 360 feedback is used as **input to the manager's review** but is not the sole basis for the rating.
- For L1 and L2 employees, peer feedback is gathered informally by the manager.

#### Step 3: Manager Review & Rating (March 16 - April 7)

- Managers review self-assessments, 360 feedback (where applicable), quarterly check-in notes, and project outcomes.
- Managers assign a performance rating using the 5-point scale (see Section 4).
- Managers draft written feedback including: strengths, development areas, and specific examples supporting the rating.

#### Step 4: Calibration (April 1 - April 7)

- Department heads and HR conduct **calibration sessions** to ensure rating consistency across teams.
- Calibration reviews the distribution of ratings to prevent grade inflation or deflation.
- Target distribution guidelines (not strict quotas):
  - Rating 5 (Exceptional): ~5% of employees
  - Rating 4 (Exceeds Expectations): ~20%
  - Rating 3 (Meets Expectations): ~55%
  - Rating 2 (Needs Improvement): ~15%
  - Rating 1 (Below Expectations): ~5%

#### Step 5: Review Meeting & Communication (April 8 - April 15)

- Managers hold a **45-60 minute review meeting** with each direct report.
- The meeting covers: final rating, written feedback, compensation changes, and goals for the next period.
- Employees sign the review acknowledgment in BambooHR within **5 business days** of the meeting.
- Employees may submit a written response or appeal within **10 business days** (see Section 6).

---

### 4. Performance Rating Scale

| Rating | Label | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **5** | Exceptional | Consistently delivers outstanding results far exceeding expectations. Demonstrates significant impact beyond their role. Role model for others. Reserved for truly extraordinary performance. |
| **4** | Exceeds Expectations | Frequently exceeds goals and expectations. Delivers high-quality work with minimal guidance. Proactively identifies and solves problems. |
| **3** | Meets Expectations | Consistently meets all role expectations and goals. Reliable, competent, and collaborative. The standard for solid, professional performance. |
| **2** | Needs Improvement | Partially meets expectations. Some goals were missed or work quality was inconsistent. A development plan should be created with specific improvement targets. |
| **1** | Below Expectations | Fails to meet minimum role requirements. Immediate intervention required. A formal Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) will be initiated. |

---

### 5. Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

A PIP is initiated when an employee receives a rating of **1 (Below Expectations)** or demonstrates sustained underperformance across 2 consecutive quarters.

#### 5.1 PIP Structure

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Duration | **60 calendar days** |
| Check-in frequency | **Bi-weekly** (every 2 weeks) with direct manager |
| Documentation | PIP Agreement signed by employee, manager, and HR |
| Success criteria | Specific, measurable objectives defined at PIP start |
| Support provided | Additional coaching, training, or mentorship as needed |

#### 5.2 PIP Process

1. **Initiation Meeting (Day 0):** Manager and HR meet with the employee to explain the PIP, set objectives, and sign the PIP Agreement.
2. **Bi-weekly Check-ins (Days 14, 28, 42, 56):** Manager meets with the employee to review progress against PIP objectives. Check-in notes are documented in BambooHR and shared with HR.
3. **Mid-Point Review (Day 30):** A formal mid-point assessment is conducted with HR present. The PIP may be adjusted based on progress.
4. **Final Review (Day 60):** Manager and HR evaluate whether the employee has met PIP objectives.

#### 5.3 PIP Outcomes

| Outcome | Action |
|---------|--------|
| **Objectives Met** | PIP closed. Employee returns to normal performance management. A follow-up review is conducted at Day 120. |
| **Partial Progress** | PIP may be extended by **30 days maximum** (one extension only). |
| **Objectives Not Met** | Employment may be terminated in accordance with the employment contract and Vietnamese Labor Law. A minimum of **30 days' notice** or pay in lieu is provided. |

---

### 6. Promotion Criteria

Promotions are recommended during the annual review cycle and require:

- A **minimum of 2 consecutive annual ratings of "Exceeds Expectations" (4) or higher**.
- Demonstration of competencies at the next level for at least 6 months.
- Manager recommendation supported by specific evidence.
- Approval by the department head and HR during calibration.
- For promotions to L4 (Lead) or above, a **promotion panel review** including at least 2 senior leaders outside the employee's direct chain is required.

Employees who have been on a PIP within the last **12 months** are not eligible for promotion.

---

### 7. Appeals Process

- Employees who disagree with their performance rating may submit a **written appeal** to HR within **10 business days** of receiving their review.
- Appeals are reviewed by the employee's skip-level manager and an HR Business Partner.
- The appeals committee will respond within **15 business days** with a final decision.
- The appeals process does not delay any compensation adjustments; if the rating is changed, compensation is adjusted retroactively.
