## Q3 2025 Revenue Report

**Prepared by:** Finance Department, Gravity Engineering Solutions JSC  
**Report Period:** July 1 - September 30, 2025  
**Date Issued:** October 15, 2025  
**Classification:** Internal - Confidential

---

### Executive Summary

Gravity Engineering Solutions recorded total revenue of **45 billion VND** in Q3 2025, representing a quarter-over-quarter (QoQ) growth of **+12%** and a year-over-year (YoY) growth of **+34%**. The strong performance was driven primarily by expanded engineering services contracts in the mining and infrastructure sectors, along with increased consulting engagements across the Asia-Pacific region.

Net profit for the quarter reached **8.5 billion VND**, reflecting disciplined cost management and favorable project mix. The company's cash position stands at **32 billion VND** as of September 30, 2025.

---

### Revenue Breakdown by Service Line

| Service Line           | Revenue (VND) | Share of Total | QoQ Change |
|------------------------|---------------|----------------|------------|
| Engineering Services   | 28,000,000,000 (28B) | 62.2%  | +15%       |
| Consulting             | 12,000,000,000 (12B) | 26.7%  | +8%        |
| Support Contracts      | 5,000,000,000 (5B)   | 11.1%  | +6%        |
| **Total**              | **45,000,000,000 (45B)** | **100%** | **+12%** |

Engineering Services remains the largest contributor at 28 billion VND, accounting for 62.2% of total revenue. This segment grew 15% QoQ due to two major infrastructure projects that entered the execution phase in August 2025. Consulting revenue reached 12 billion VND, supported by demand for digital transformation advisory in the retail and logistics sectors. Support Contracts generated 5 billion VND in recurring revenue with a 94% renewal rate.

### Outlook for Q4 2025

Management projects Q4 2025 revenue between 48-50 billion VND based on the current pipeline, representing continued sequential growth. Key drivers include the ramp-up of two signed consulting contracts and expected closure of three engineering services opportunities currently in the proposal stage. The headcount is expected to grow to 325 by year-end to support expanding project commitments.
