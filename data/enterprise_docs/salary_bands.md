---

### 1. Purpose

This document defines the compensation framework for all full-time employees of GravityTech Vietnam. It establishes transparent salary bands, bonus structures, and review processes aligned with our commitment to equitable and competitive compensation in the Vietnamese technology market.

Salary benchmarking is conducted annually against Mercer, Robert Walters, and Navigos Group market data for the Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi technology sectors.

---

### 2. Level Structure & Salary Bands

GravityTech uses a 5-level career framework. Each level has a defined salary band expressed in gross monthly salary (Vietnamese Dong - VND).

| Level | Title | Monthly Gross Salary (VND) | Typical Experience |
|-------|-------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| **L1** | Junior / Associate | 15,000,000 - 22,000,000 | 0-2 years |
| **L2** | Mid-Level / Specialist | 22,000,000 - 35,000,000 | 2-5 years |
| **L3** | Senior / Staff | 35,000,000 - 55,000,000 | 5-8 years |
| **L4** | Lead / Principal | 55,000,000 - 80,000,000 | 8-12 years |
| **L5** | Director / VP | 80,000,000 - 120,000,000 | 12+ years |

**Notes on salary bands:**
- Salary bands represent the full range. New hires typically enter between the 25th and 50th percentile of the band for their level.
- Employees at or above the 90th percentile of their band are generally expected to pursue promotion to the next level.
- Exceptions above band maximum require written approval from the CPO and CFO.
- These bands apply to all functions (Engineering, Product, Design, Operations, Sales, HR, Finance). Function-specific differentials of up to +/- 10% may apply based on market data.

### 3. Salary Components

#### 3.1 Base Salary
- Paid monthly by the **5th of each month** via bank transfer.
- Subject to statutory deductions: Personal Income Tax (PIT), Social Insurance (SI - 8%), Health Insurance (HI - 1.5%), Unemployment Insurance (UI - 1%).

#### 3.2 13th Month Salary
- All employees receive a **13th month salary** equivalent to one month of base salary.
- Paid in **December** each year, pro-rated for employees who joined after January 1.
- For employees joining after July 1, the 13th month salary is calculated as: (months worked / 12) x monthly base salary.
- The 13th month salary is a guaranteed payment and is not dependent on company or individual performance.

#### 3.3 Performance Bonus
- An annual performance bonus of **0 to 3 months' base salary** is awarded based on the annual performance review rating.

| Performance Rating | Bonus (Months of Salary) |
|-------------------|--------------------------|
| 1 - Below Expectations | 0 months |
| 2 - Needs Improvement | 0.5 months |
| 3 - Meets Expectations | 1 month |
| 4 - Exceeds Expectations | 2 months |
| 5 - Exceptional | 3 months |

- Performance bonuses are paid in **May**, following the annual review cycle in April.
- Employees must have completed at least **6 months of service** by December 31 to be eligible for a performance bonus.
- Employees on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) at the time of the annual review are not eligible for a performance bonus.

#### 3.4 Project & Spot Bonuses
- Managers may nominate employees for spot bonuses of up to **5,000,000 VND** for exceptional contributions.
- Project completion bonuses are determined on a case-by-case basis by the project sponsor and approved by Finance.

### 4. Annual Review & Salary Adjustment Cycle

| Activity | Timeline |
|----------|----------|
| Self-assessment submission | March 1 - March 15 |
| Manager review & calibration | March 16 - March 31 |
| Calibration committee (L4+ leadership) | April 1 - April 7 |
| Final ratings communicated | April 8 - April 15 |
| Salary adjustments effective | **April 1** (retroactive) |
| Performance bonuses paid | **May 5** |

#### 4.1 Cost of Living Adjustment (COLA)
- A cost of living adjustment of **5% to 8%** is applied annually, effective April 1.
- The exact COLA percentage is determined by the Finance and People teams based on:
  - Vietnam Consumer Price Index (CPI) for the preceding 12 months.
  - Company financial performance.
  - Market salary movement data.
- COLA is applied to base salary before any merit-based increases.

#### 4.2 Merit Increases
- Merit increases are awarded on top of COLA based on performance rating:

| Performance Rating | Merit Increase |
|-------------------|----------------|
| 1 - Below Expectations | 0% |
| 2 - Needs Improvement | 0-2% |
| 3 - Meets Expectations | 3-5% |
| 4 - Exceeds Expectations | 6-10% |
| 5 - Exceptional | 10-15% |

- Total annual salary increase (COLA + merit) is typically capped at **20%** unless accompanied by a promotion.

---

### 5. Promotion-Related Salary Adjustments

- Promotions involve a move to a higher level and a salary adjustment to at least the **minimum of the new band**.
- Typical promotion salary increase: **15-25%** of current base salary.
- Off-cycle promotions (outside the April cycle) are permitted with VP-level approval and are effective on the 1st of the month following approval.
- Employees cannot be promoted more than one level at a time except in extraordinary circumstances approved by the CEO.
